Reflection

In the Acting Green CAT, we were tasked with creating a more sustainable home. I mainly focused on electricity consumption, with all three strategies having something to do with it because of the high power consumption that we have. My implementations helped a lot with the sustainability of my household, cutting significantly on energy usage. This was done by turning off, reducing time on, and optimising the power consumption of devices, lights, and appliances. I saved on about 20 lights or appliances, 42 watts of devices' power consumption, and cut in half the amount of power that the boiler uses for showers. I also cut on water usage, as our shower lengths have been cut in half. These savings greatly increased the sustainability of our home by massively reducing power and water consumption. Going forward, we will likely keep these changes, gradually incorporating them into our daily lives. Whenever we see a light, device, or appliance that has been left on, we will switch it off. We will naturally decrease the amount of time that we spend in the shower, and we will turn on new energy-saving features on devices when we come across them.
